The Jira visibility gap

Epic time is easy to underestimate when work is logged deeper down

Jira can show time logged on Stories under an Epic, but time logged on sub-tasks below those Stories can disappear from the Epic-level view. Worlog Rollup solves that by summing logged time through the full descendant tree.

  • Open an Epic, Story or parent issue and see the total immediately.
  • Include nested children even when the hierarchy goes multiple levels down.
  • Use the included issue table to understand what contributed to the total.

FAQ

Short answers for teams evaluating logged-time rollups in Jira.

What issues are included?

The app includes the current issue and descendant issues found through Jira parent relationships, including deeper nested levels.

Why is this needed for Epics?

Time logged on Stories can be visible at the Epic level, but time logged on sub-tasks below those Stories is easy to miss. Worlog Rollup totals the full descendant tree.

What time fields are used?

The app reads Jira time tracking values such as timespent and timetracking.timeSpentSeconds.

What does MD mean?

MD means man-day. The product displays 1 MD as 8 MH.

Which languages are supported?

The app supports English, Spanish, French and Polish.

Does it support Jira dark mode?

Yes. The panel supports light and dark mode.

Does it read Tempo data?

No. The product is focused on Jira issue and time tracking data.

Does it calculate billing?

No. It does not calculate rates, costs, invoices or billing amounts.
